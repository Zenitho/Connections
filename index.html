HR Corner

Time for a vacation?
Taking time off is important to your health and well-being!

As much as we would love to spend every moment with you, Cobalt recognizes the importance of maintaining a healthy work-life balance for employees. Time away from the office helps maintain a healthy mind, promotes emotional and physical well-being, reduces stress, prevents burnout, reduces disease and illness, and promotes a returned increase in focus, motivation and productivity.

Reminder: Use your accrued vacation
time by December 31!

Some reminders as you plan your time off:

  • You must log your vacation time in Ascentis—Exempt employees must log vacation days as well.
  • Plan ahead so you do not run out of vacation time—if you plan on taking vacation in November, be sure you don't use your time up before then.
  • Cobalt does not permit unpaid time off for vacation purposes.
  • Unused, accrued vacation time at the end of a calendar year, to the extent permitted by law, will be forfeited and will not be carried over to the next year or cashed out.
  • Management must approve your vacation time prior to your vacation being taken.
  • Find your vacation balance on your paystub or by emailing Payroll.

Please address questions regarding time entry and vacation accruals to
payroll@cobaltmortgage.com. Thanks for reading and enjoy your vacation!

Behind the Screens
Get a glimpse of the IT Team that keeps you up and running
By Robin Ortiz, Manager of IT Support

Imagine if every one of Cobalt's 1,055 employees called with an IT problem simultaneously? Fortunately, that's unlikely. But we aim to be prepared to handle most any eventuality—from forgotten passwords and virus attacks to power outages and network crashes.

In the company's 13-year history, IT has grown from four people to 21 strong today. Obviously, we recruit brilliant technicians who are creative problem solvers. But believe it or not, tech expertise isn't worth much unless it's paired with excellent customer service skills. So we deliberately hire employees who have the same level of extraordinary people skills as our loan officers. We're proud of our top-notch team, and love it when you take the time to give your techs an occasional "shout out" (see this issue's "Got Raves" below).

Meet the team!


Ops. Center control room at Kirkland Way Office

How the IT team deals with stress

Pranks? Well, yes, we in the IT Department do like to have a bit of fun now and then. So imagine you are part of IT, and you are in a hurry to get to a user to help them with an issue. You run off and accidentally leave your computer unlocked.

As you know, you are duly warned "Do not leave your computer unlocked or bad things may happen." Well, if you are not at your computer and the call volume has waned, you become a likely candidate for a prank. But what to leave on the computer as a desktop background? David Hasselhoff or Richard Simmons? We make the choice, then minimize all applications so the victim knows what happened. Whatever image we choose, that person will remember to lock their computer the next time they walk away.



It's not that we are mean by nature (well, maybe a little); but we are responsible for a lot of information that can be compromised if a PC is left unlocked for the world to see. So, this our subtle reminder to each other to secure our computer before walking away and helps lighten up a sometimes stressful environment.

Helping IT help you

3 ways you can make it easier for your tech to help with your next problem:

  1. Give your name, department and PC number when you call or email IT Support.
  2. State the nature of your problem as clearly as possible.
  3. State the level of urgency of your problem.

I hope that gives you just a little glimpse "behind the screens" of our IT crew. We are continually working to improve our response times and systems to meet the needs of Cobalt's growing staff and branch offices. If you have a suggestion or idea toward that goal, feel free to drop me a line robin.ortiz@cobaltmortgage.com.

Surefire Update

Surefire FAQ of the Month - July

Q: My client didn't get put on a post-close campaign. What happened?

A: The post-close campaign gets automatically added to a contact when the loan funds; that is, when the status shows "funded" in Point/Processing. Since Surefire only syncs with Point/Processing, once the loan is moved to the Point/Closed folder, it stops syncing. After the loan closes and the status shows "funded," if the loan is moved to the closed folder before the nightly (1:00 a.m. ET) sync, then Surefire can't recognized its "funded" status. Consequently, the post-close campaign does not get added.
